Project Idea

Almost every modern phone nowadays has an inbuilt NFC chip which in my opinion does not get enough use by your average phone user. The idea behind this project is to start using the mobile phones NFC capabilities to full extent while getting rid of some old-fashioned devices and accessories in order to make persons everyday life smoother and quicker. Present-day access to facilities and houses is overcomplicated by the number of things you need to have on person to enter. To be more specific it’s the key-card and key system that I see as a problem. There is 1,214,372 (2016*) separate apartments (including flats and units) in the whole Australia and vast majority of them use the key-card plus normal household key system. In my opinion that is too many keys and key-cards to be carried around everyday which could be easily replaced by one thing that is almost always with you when you go somewhere: the mobile phone! *Abs.gov.au. (2019). 2071.0 - Census of Population and Housing: Reflecting Australia - Stories from the Census, 2016. [online] Available at: https://www.abs.gov.au/ausstats/abs@.nsf/Lookup/by%20Subject/2071.0~2016~Main%20Features~Apartment%20Living~20 [Accessed 31 Mar. 2019]. The idea is fairly simple, you touch your phone against the key-card reader or intercom at the entrance and through NFC the household security system recognises your phone and gives you further access and then repeat once at the apartment door itself. Now this is not all as it leaves too many security and safety issues, for example what if your phone gets stolen and that someone tries to access your apartment when you’re out. In order to remove this problem a mobile phone APP needs to be developed which then would co-operate with the system and ask the person for a PIN-code or password making it harder for the criminal to get past the door. Even now it is still not secure enough for some people’s tastes as the robber can find out the PIN. This is where the 2-step verification comes in to make sure clients belongings are safe. The second step would involve a use of biometric authentication such as face scan or fingerprint scan, both of which are more and more often implemented in smartphones coming to markets these days. Now that there is biometric security implemented no thief will be able to easily access the clients dwelling. The idea of this project does not only make everyday activity of entering ones home quicker and easier but at the same time also gets rid off over 2.5 million keys and key-cards that are needed to be carried around everyday replacement of which (in case of damage or loss) also costs ridiculous amounts of money to lessees and landlords. I won’t even be able to fully describe the environmental impact of printing all those plastic cards to replace the lost ones. The intercom systems would only require an update to assure the compatibility with the App making it ready to be used without any physical changes. There are several initial drawbacks when it comes to this project and the major one being that not every phone has NFC built-in. However, that is not too big of a problem as in that case NFC stickers can be put on the back of the phone which won’t be too noticeable or thick and would weigh way less than the combination of a key and key-card. Secondly, a mobile App will need to be designed and developed to facilitate the PIN-code and biometric authentication security measures mentioned earlier. This is doable either through putting an order in with a third-party company that offers such services of creating Apps, which adds some guarantee of service, or by writing the software ourselves. The second method is likely to result in an App that has bugs and is not as polished as the end-user might prefer. Mostly because the team is going to be consisting of people still getting their bachelor’s degrees who also have minimal experience in the field. Hardest problem to solve would be the fact that even though biometric scanners are implemented in many phones there are still large numbers of ones without the scanners. The only solution as of now is to use the already existing intercom systems to capture the face and compare it with the picture on the server. Overall project will have several outcomes affecting both people’s everyday lives and environment. Entering the household would become hassle-free experience with only one piece of equipment required, which person carries in their pocket almost always anyways. The intercoms have already been discussed and nothing much will change when it comes to those. The reduced waste from lost keys or cards preserves the nature and reduces footprint we leave on our planet. And of course, the most important part is where the person does not need to stress about forgetting their keys and getting locked out because one thing that almost no-one ever forgets is: their phone!
